Inaccurate Sexual Health Information

Did you know that only 19 states require that sexual health information taught in schools be considered medically accurate as of 2014? Many women are misinformed about their sexual health, preventing them from seeking the care they need.


Learn more about the issues in modern sex education at the following links:

National Conference of State Legislatures (NCSL) · Guttmacher Institute · Force Change (Petition to End Abstinence Only Education)


Follow these links to learn about how poor sexual health education on the part of either the patient or doctor can put LGBTQ+ identifying individuals at higher risk of receiving insufficient sexual health care:

American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists · National Center for Transgender Equality

Legal Barriers

There have been many recent policies within the United States that have reduced accessibility to not just procedures such as abortion but to sexual health care in general. For example, some of these policies are designed to make sexual health care more expensive, or less available

Check out the links below to learn what policies serve as barriers to those seeking sexual health care, and what action is being taken against these policies.

National Women's Law Center ·Planned Parenthood in Action ·Medscape







Unsafe Abortion Clinics

Did you know that approximately 68,000 women die of unsafe abortion annually, making it one of the leading causes of maternal mortality?


Learn more about unsafe abortions by following these links:

Crisis Pregnancy Centers

Many women seeking reproductive health care encounter crisis pregnancy centers. While some of these centers provide actual health care, many of these centers strongly discourage patients from pursuing certain reproductive choices including engaging in sexual activity, using birth control, and undergoing abortion.
